Play? At school?


You bet.

Kids need time to just play and be kids, while still needing structure, and organized play at school is vital to the holistic education of all students.

 

So what's happening to play?


With the School District of Philadelphia looking at an ever increasing budget problem, non-core activities are being eliminated across the district.

Even with Andrew Jackson increasing its student body every year, the cuts are real, and they are getting deeper.

 

So who is Coach Dave?


Dave is a coach from Playworks who is with the kids at Andrew Jackson every day. He organizes recess time and structured play for the K-8 kids at Jackson.

Dave is a rockstar to the kids.

 

There is hope.

Non-profit groups, in conjunction with Andrew Jackson and Playworks, are working to keep coaches and mentors in schools and to keep organized recreational play a reality

But it doesn't have to be this way

The Friends of Jackson and the PSCA want to ensure kids are safe and learning at school.

Remember when you were 9?
Were you King of the Hill, or Queen of Kickball?

You remember the time spent with your friends on the playground at recess. You would spend time playing games for some time during the day, then go back to class with the "wiggles" out and ready for more learning. That's the way it used to be.

Now students get time only for lunch and are asked to sit in classrooms for seven hours a day. Where is the time to be a kid?

Education does not end when a student is away from the desk, and that educating the whole child lays the foundation for years of learning and success.

A school district on life support,
and the worst is yet to come.

However, reality now is that the School District of Philadelphia is eliminating programs and curricula that are not deemed "core" by the State of Pennsylvania. Those "core" areas are ultimately what funding is based on, and in the end the students are the losers here, and the loss is very real.

As of December 2013, the School District of Philadelphia was still facing a budget gap of a staggering $304 million with no relief in sight. As the District's Chief Financial Officer recently stated: "Right now we are in a funding situation where we are unable to provide basic educational services for students". 4

Playworks and Coach Dave,
letting kids be kids.

Andrew Jackson School has a student mix of over a dozen ethnicities and varied socioeconomic backgrounds. This mix can be difficult for both the students and teachers, and that's there organized play becomes vital. Kids learning to cooperate together and understand how to relate to each other on the playground translates to the classroom where the improvements are real.

Playworks is an organization dedicated to creating safe and organized play for over 425,000 5 students this year using trained volunteers and coaches. Andrew Jackson School was lucky to get Dave in 2012, and his work with the kids has been transformative.

Playworks' own surveys to PA school staff bears this out in real numbers that equate to real learning improvements.
